About

From the classroom to the rulebook.

I build the rule-based logic that healthcare and finance platforms run their decisions on. At Notable Systems, I architect the boolean rules and LLM prompts behind automated approval/denial recommendations, holding 95% accuracy across client implementations — then write the Python and shell scripts to test that logic at scale before it ships.

Most of the job is translation: a client hands over a page of criteria written for a human reviewer, and I turn it into logic that runs correctly, every time, without a person in the loop. That instinct for turning ambiguous rules into something precise goes back to teaching first grade, and sharpened further through UX research — my senior project, LinguaGemma, won a CHI undergraduate research award for studying how people actually learn from a system, not just how the system was designed to teach.

The same instinct shows up on the creative side too: building playable, interactive 3D work in Unity and Three.js, where the "rules" are game mechanics and interaction logic instead of claims criteria — same precision, different material.

I'm looking for the next version of this work: logic development, rule engineering, and AI prompt roles; UX and game/interactive design roles; or project/product management roles where translating ambiguous requirements into a plan people can actually execute is the job.
